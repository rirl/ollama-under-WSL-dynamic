= KIOS Domain Catalog
:toc:
:sectnums:

KIOS is organized around domains.

A domain is the primary unit of durable knowledge within KIOS. It defines a
coherent body of concepts, relationships, policies, evidence, validation, and
provenance concerning a particular subject.

Implementations demonstrate domains.

They do not define them.

As implementations evolve, are replaced, or become obsolete, domains preserve
the conceptual knowledge extracted from those implementations together with the
reasoning that justifies it.

Accordingly, KIOS is fundamentally a knowledge architecture rather than a
software architecture.

== Purpose

The Domain Catalog serves as the canonical entry point into the KIOS domain
architecture.

Its purposes are to:

* identify the canonical domains
* provide stable identifiers
* describe domain maturity
* identify reference implementations
* establish a common architectural vocabulary
* organize future knowledge development

== Canonical Domain Model

Every mature KIOS domain is expected to describe these architectural elements.

=== Vocabulary

The canonical language of the domain.

=== Concepts

The primary entities of the domain.

=== Relationships

The semantic structure connecting concepts.

=== Policies

Declarative rules governing acceptable behavior and valid transitions.

=== Evidence

Observable facts supporting claims made by the domain.

=== Warrant

Reasoning that justifies conclusions drawn from evidence.

=== Validation

Procedures demonstrating that reasoning and implementations satisfy the
declared policies.

=== Reference Implementations

Concrete systems demonstrating that the domain model is executable.

Reference implementations produce evidence.

They are not the domain.

=== Provenance

The historical record describing how the domain evolved.

== Domain Lifecycle

Domains mature through evidence.

----
Problem
    ↓
Observation
    ↓
Research
    ↓
Hypothesis
    ↓
Architecture
    ↓
Reference Implementation
    ↓
Validation
    ↓
Canonical Domain
----

Promotion between stages should always be justified by evidence and warrant.

== Repository Organization

A representative domain organization is:

----
domains/
    README.adoc

    <domain>/
        0001-DOMAIN.adoc

        concepts/
        relationships/
        policies/
        validation/
        implementations/
        provenance/
        artifacts/
----

The organization exists to preserve durable knowledge.

It should not mirror implementation details unless those details themselves are
part of the domain.

== Architectural Principles

KIOS is organized around several principles.

=== Domains Are Durable

Implementations evolve.

Domains accumulate knowledge.

=== Evidence Precedes Warrant

Observations alone are insufficient.

Reasoned justification is required before knowledge becomes canonical.

=== Provenance Preserves Understanding

Knowledge is not merely the current conclusion.

Knowledge includes the history of how that conclusion was reached.

=== Validation Produces Confidence

Domains should be promoted only after sufficient validation has accumulated.

=== Reference Implementations Produce Evidence

Repositories demonstrate the usefulness of domains.

They do not replace domains.

== Long-Term Architecture

KIOS naturally separates into three complementary layers.

----
Reference Implementations
        │
        ▼
Canonical Domains
        │
        ▼
KIOS Platform
----

Reference implementations generate evidence.

Canonical domains synthesize durable knowledge from that evidence.

The KIOS platform reasons over domains, evaluates evidence, enforces policy,
preserves provenance, and assists users in constructing new knowledge.

This separation allows implementations to evolve independently while preserving
the long-term integrity of the knowledge architecture.
